This page shows you how to use our Braille-to-text converter

  1. Choose or take an image of Braille that you would like to translate into English
    2. A sample picture of Braille taken from a library book

    Save the image on your computer

  2. Crop your image
    3. A sample picture of good cropping

    Our braille-to-text converter uses a ratio to segment and translate each character, so it is essential that you comply with the following cropping instructions in order to get a good translation. Crop your image so that there is about as much padding as the image above (refer to the red line showing the padding). The size of the padding should be about half of the amount of space between two Braille characters.

  3. Go to the 'Upload Image' page and upload your image by clicking the 'Choose File' button
    4. Choosing a file to upload
  4. Click on the 'Submit' button and you should see the translation below!
    NOTE:
  • Our Braille-to-text-converter only translates English Grade 1 Braille
  • So far we can only translate one line of Braille at a time. If you would like to translate multiple lines of Braille, please upload each line separately.
  • We trained our model only on embossed Braille so pictures of handwritten Braille might not translate well
